Dr. Lilia Ann Abron

(1930)

First black woman to earn a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering in 1972

Alice Ball

(1892 - 1916)

First African American and woman to graduate with a M.S. in Chemistry

Developed the "Ball Method" which was the most effective treatment for leprosy

Dr. Patricia Bath

(1942 - 2019)

Ophthalmologist who is the inventor of laser cataract surgery

First black female doctor to secure a medical patent

Dr. Alexa Canady

(1950)

First black female Neurosurgeon in the United States in 1981

Bessie Coleman

(1892-1926)

First Black and Native American woman to hold an international pilots license

Dr. Rebecca Lee Crumpler

(1831 - 1895)

First black woman to become a physician in 1864

Dr. Marie M. Daly

(1921–2003)

First black woman to earn a Ph.D. in Chemisty in 1943

Euphemia Haynes

(1890-1980)

First black woman to earn a Ph.D. in Mathematics in 1943

Dr. Christine Darden

(1942)

One of the world's experts on sonic boom prediction, sonic boom minimization and supersonic wing design throughout her 40 year career at NASA

Mary Jackson

(1921 - 2015)

A mathematician who became NASA's first Black female engineer in 1958.
Specialized in Aerospace engineering

Dr. Shirley Jackson

(1946)

Inventor of the Touch-Tone Telephone, Called ID and the Fiber-Optic Cable

Dr. Mae C. Jemison

(1956)

First black female NASA astronaut to travel to space in 1992

Katherine Johnson

(1918 - 2020)

Mathematician whose calculations of orbital mechanics for NASA were critical to the success of the first and subsequent U.S. crewed spaceflights.

Dr. Willie Hobbs Moore

(1934 - 1994)

First black woman to earn a Ph.D. in Physics in 1972

Mabel Keaton Staupers

(1890 - 1989)

A Caribbean-American nurse who was an integral part in eliminating segregation in the Armed Forces Nurse Corps during World War II as well as other educational, institutional, and organizational structures

Hattie Scott Peterson

(1913 - 1993)

The first black woman to hold a civil engineering degree in 1946.

Later became the first female engineer at U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE)

Valerie Thomas

(1943)

NASA physicist who invented the illusion transmitter which allows us to watch 3D Movies and Television

Dr. Gladys West

(1930)

Mathematician whose accurate mathematical modeling of Earth lead the GPS

Dr. Marguerite Williams

(1895-1991)

First African American to earn a Ph.D. in Geology in 1942

Dorothy Vaughan

(1910 - 2008)

A mathematician and programmer with expertise in FORTRAN became the first African American manager at NASA in 1958
